# Assessment Context: Online Assessment Center (OAC) 2024
## Kementerian Keuangan RI – Direktorat Jenderal Pajak
### Target Position: Pejabat Administrator (Batch 42)

---

## CRITICAL RULES FOR ASSESSMENT

1. **All 9 competencies have SKJ = Level 3** for Administrator position
2. **Integritas minimum is Level 3** — a score of 1 or 2 in Integritas is unacceptable
3. Other competencies may score Level 2 or even 1, but Level 3 is the target
4. Assessment is based on BEHAVIORAL EVIDENCE across all AC activities — not just the narrative/CI alone
5. The Critical Incident (CI) is a supporting tool, not the sole basis for scoring

---

## JOB PERSON MATCH (JPM)

JPM = (Employee's achieved level ÷ SKJ level) × 100%, averaged across all 9 competencies

| Category | JPM Range |
|---|---|
| Optimal | ≥ 90% |
| Cukup Optimal (Kemenkeu standard) | ≥ 80% |
| Kurang Optimal | < 78% |

**Practical JPM examples for Administrator (all SKJ = 3):**
- All competencies Level 3 → JPM = 100%
- One competency Level 2 → JPM ≈ 96.3%
- Two competencies Level 2 → JPM ≈ 92.6%
- Three competencies Level 2 → JPM ≈ 88.9%

## PROFICIENCY LEVEL GUIDE

| Level | Label | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dasar | Needs guidance; follows instructions |
| 2 | Menengah | Works independently; can coach others on basics |
| 3 | Mumpuni | Evaluates processes; develops methods; leads without guidance — **TARGET for Administrator** |
| 4 | Ahli | Creates concepts/policy; primary organizational reference |
| 5 | Paham | Strategic/national level influence |

**Level 3 (Mumpuni) is the key threshold.** Look for evidence that the person: leads a team, evaluates and improves processes, develops methods, and guides others without needing direction.

## COMPETENCY DEFINITIONS & LEVEL 3 INDICATORS

### 1. Integritas
**Definition:** Consistent behavior aligned with organizational values, norms, and ethics; honest with all stakeholders; accountable for decisions and risks.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Ensures team members act in line with organizational ethics in all situations
- Provides recognition and correction to uphold ethical standards
- Acts as a role model for integrity; monitors and evaluates ethical practices in the unit

### 2. Kerja Sama
**Definition:** Building and maintaining effective working relationships; mutual commitment to task completion; optimizing resources for strategic goals.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Identifies team strengths and weaknesses; leverages them for performance
- Prioritizes decisions based on team member input
- Builds alliances with external stakeholders to achieve organizational goals

### 3. Komunikasi
**Definition:** Clearly and systematically conveying ideas with logical arguments; active listening; persuading and influencing others toward organizational goals.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Communicates sensitive or complex information appropriately
- Simplifies complex topics for various audiences
- Produces annual/periodic reports and formal communications independently

### 4. Orientasi pada Hasil
**Definition:** High personal commitment to completing tasks; accountability; systematically identifying risks and opportunities aligned with planning and results.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Sets challenging performance targets for the unit (above organizational baseline)
- Provides recognition and correction to drive achievement
- Develops more effective work methods for the unit

### 5. Pelayanan Publik
**Definition:** Professional, transparent, objective, and non-discriminatory execution of government and public service duties.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Explains policy implications and their impact on public service
- Develops systems or processes to improve public service delivery
- Understands and explains interdependencies among public service components

### 6. Pengembangan Diri dan Orang Lain
**Definition:** Continuously improving own knowledge and skills; inspiring others; providing guidance, feedback, and coaching to develop others' potential.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Assigns challenging tasks as learning opportunities for subordinates
- Observes subordinates and provides objective, constructive feedback
- Conducts structured development discussions with team members

### 7. Mengelola Perubahan
**Definition:** Adapting to new/changing situations; not reliant on old methods; supporting and leading change initiatives; accountable for effective implementation.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Helps others adapt to change
- Adjusts work priorities in response to change
- Provides effective solutions to problems arising from change

### 8. Pengambilan Keputusan
**Definition:** Timely, confident decision-making based on careful consideration, systematic analysis, multiple information sources, alternatives, and accountability.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Compares multiple courses of action and their implications
- Selects the best solution based on systematic analysis
- Balances risk and success probability in implementation

### 9. Perekat Bangsa (Social-Cultural)
**Definition:** Promoting tolerance and openness; bridging diversity; building social and psychological connections within Indonesia's pluralistic society.

**Level 3 indicators (target):**
- Promotes attitudes of respect for differences
- Conducts social mapping within the work environment
- Acts as a mediator to resolve or prevent conflicts arising from diversity

## WHAT A STRONG LEVEL 3 NARRATIVE LOOKS LIKE

A narrative that earns Level 3 typically shows:
- **Leading, not just doing** — the person guides or directs a team, not just performing individual tasks
- **Evaluating and improving** — actively identifies what's not working and changes methods
- **Organizational impact** — actions affect the unit or team, not just personal performance
- **Handling complexity** — deals with difficult situations, competing priorities, or interpersonal challenges
- **Concrete results** — outcomes are measurable or clearly described

A narrative that stays at Level 2 typically shows:
- Good individual performance, but no clear team leadership
- Follows processes well, but doesn't evaluate or redesign them
- Helps others informally, but no systematic development or coaching

## ABOUT CRITICAL INCIDENT (CI)

The CI is a pre-AC document where the assessee describes:
- The most meaningful work situation experienced in the **last 3 years**
- Steps taken and results achieved, including how setbacks were handled

**For assessment purposes:**
- CI is a supporting reference, not the sole evidence
- It should contain personal, first-person behavioral examples
- Vague or generic responses reduce credibility
- Assessors also observe behavior across all AC simulations (case analysis, presentation, group discussion, role play, interview)
